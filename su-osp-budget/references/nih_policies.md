# NIH-Specific Policies

Policies and requirements specific to National Institutes of Health awards.

## NIH Salary Cap

### Current Cap Levels

**Effective January 1, 2025 - September 30, 2025:** $225,700  
**Effective October 1, 2024 - December 31, 2024:** $221,900

The cap is tied to **Executive Level II** of the Federal Executive Pay Scale and typically updates twice per year (October 1 and January 1).

### Policy Changes (FY2025+)

**NEW:** As of FY2025 (awards issued Oct 1, 2024+), the salary cap applies to:
- **Direct salaries** (individuals working on NIH projects)
- **Indirect salaries** (executive salaries in uncapped cost pools)

Previously (through FY2024), cap applied only to direct salaries.

### When Cap Applies

**Subject to cap:**
- All personnel charged directly to NIH grants and cooperative agreements
- Research grants (R01, R21, R15, etc.)
- Program project grants (P01, P30, etc.)
- Applies to actual salary, exclusive of fringe and F&A

**Not subject to cap:**
- NIH contracts (use FAR instead)
- Institutional funds used for cost sharing
- Fringe benefits and F&A (calculated on capped or actual amount per regulations)

### Calculating Mandatory Cost Sharing

When faculty salary exceeds the NIH cap:

**Example: Faculty with $300,000 AY salary, requesting 2 months summer**

1. Calculate actual 2-month salary: $300,000 ÷ 8.5 × 2 = $70,588.24
2. Calculate capped amount: $225,700 ÷ 12 × 2 = $37,616.67
3. Cost sharing required: $70,588.24 - $37,616.67 = $32,971.57

**Charged to NIH:**
- Salary (capped): $37,616.67
- Fringe (29.5%): $11,096.92
- F&A (49.5%): $24,113.22
- **NIH Total: $72,826.81**

**Mandatory Cost Share (University pays, typically Fund 11):**
- Salary (excess): $32,971.57
- Fringe (37.5% on Fund 11): $12,364.34
- **Cost Share Total: $45,335.91**

**Key points:**
- Cost sharing uses higher fringe rate (Fund 11: 37.5%)
- NO F&A charged on cost-shared portion
- Must be documented in budget and justified
- Verifiable from institutional payroll records

### CRITICAL: Salary Cap Cost Sharing Does NOT Count Toward Matching

**Important distinction:** Mandatory cost sharing from NIH salary cap **CANNOT be used to meet cost sharing or matching requirements** specified by the sponsor.

**Example scenario:**
- Sponsor requires 10% cost sharing ($50,000)
- Faculty salary exceeds NIH cap, creating $40,000 mandatory cost sharing
- That $40,000 does NOT count toward the required $50,000 match
- Must provide separate $50,000 cost sharing from other sources

**Why:** Per 2 CFR 200 and Syracuse University policy, costs representing salaries over regulatory caps cannot be used to meet cost sharing commitments. The cap-related cost sharing is a separate institutional obligation.

## NRSA Stipend Levels (Training & Fellowship Grants)

### For T32, F31, F32, and other Training/Fellowship Awards

**Predoctoral:** $28,788/year ($2,399/month)

**Postdoctoral (by experience):**
- Postdoc 0: $62,232/year ($5,186/month)
- Postdoc 1: $62,652/year ($5,221/month)
- Postdoc 2: $63,120/year ($5,260/month)
- Postdoc 3: $65,640/year ($5,470/month)
- Postdoc 4: $67,824/year ($5,652/month)
- Postdoc 5: $70,344/year ($5,862/month)
- Postdoc 6: $72,960/year ($6,080/month)
- Postdoc 7+: $75,564/year ($6,297/month)

### Training Grant Budget Components

**F&A:**
- Limited to **8% of MTDC** (not 49.5%)

**Tuition:**
- Predoctoral: 60% up to $16,000/year
- Postdoctoral: 60% up to $4,500/year

**Institutional Allowance:**
- Predoctoral: $4,750/year (non-federal institutions)
- Postdoctoral: $12,400/year (non-federal institutions)

**Other:**
- Childcare: Up to $3,000 per budget period
- Travel: $1,000 per trainee (NIAID cap)

## Research Grants vs. Training Grants

### NIH Research Grants (R01, R21, R15, etc.)

**Graduate Students:**
- Use Syracuse unionized RA rates ($28,750 for PhD in FY26)
- Full F&A applies (49.5%)
- Tuition per college policy (100% A&S remission)

**Postdocs:**
- Use competitive market rates ($60,405+ to meet NYS threshold)
- Full F&A applies (49.5%)
- Fringe: 29.5%

### NIH Training Grants (T32, F31, F32, etc.)

**Trainees:**
- Must use NRSA stipend levels
- F&A limited to 8%
- Tuition: 60% up to caps

## Important NIH Distinctions

**Salary vs. Stipend:**
- Salary = compensation for work (employees, subject to taxes)
- Stipend = support for training/education (trainees, different tax treatment)

**Research vs. Training:**
- Research grants: PI conducts research, hires staff
- Training grants: Support trainee development, prescribed stipends

**Direct vs. Indirect Costs:**
- Direct: Costs specifically for the project
- Indirect (F&A): Institutional costs (facilities, administration)
- NIH caps vary by mechanism (49.5% for research, 8% for training)

## NIH Graduate Student Compensation Cap on Research Grants

**Per NOT-OD-02-017 (still in effect):**

Total graduate student compensation on NIH research grants (R01, R21, R15, etc.) is capped at the **postdoctoral Year 0 NRSA stipend level** at time of award.

**FY2025 Cap:** $62,232 (subject to annual adjustment)

### What Counts Toward the Cap

The cap applies to TOTAL compensation:
- Salary/wages charged to the grant
- Fringe benefits
- Tuition remission (whether charged or institutionally funded)

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: School of Education Students on NIH Grants

**Problem:** Education graduate students on full-time appointments with 100% tuition charged to NIH grants exceed the $62,232 compensation cap by approximately $6,000.

**Solutions:**

**Option 1: Reduce stipend (not recommended)**
- Reduce stipend to approximately $21,500 to stay under cap
- Issue: May violate union minimum compensation requirements
- Issue: May not provide living wage for students

**Option 2: Part-time appointment (recommended)**
- Reduce to 0.75 FTE or less (15 hours/week instead of 20)
- Prorate all costs accordingly
- Stays within cap while maintaining reasonable compensation
- Example: 0.75 FTE = $21,563 stipend + $2,329 fringe + $27,203 tuition = $51,095 total

**Option 3: Share costs with non-NIH funding**
- Charge partial stipend to NIH (stay under cap)
- Supplement from internal funds, training grants, or non-federal sources
- Requires coordination with multiple funding sources

### Before Budgeting Education Students on NIH Grants

**Consult with:**
1. **Graduate School** - Ensure compliance with minimum compensation policies
2. **Graduate Union (SGEU)** - Verify union contract requirements for part-time appointments
3. **Office of Sponsored Programs** - Confirm compliance strategy before proposal submission

### Note: Training Grants vs Research Grants

This cap applies to NIH **research grants** (R-series: R01, R21, R15, etc.).

NIH **training grants** (T32, F31, F32) use prescribed NRSA stipend levels and have different rules:
- Predoctoral NRSA: $28,788/year (FY2025)
- Postdoctoral NRSA: $62,232/year (Year 0, FY2025)
- Tuition allowance: 60% up to $16,000/year for predoctoral

See "NRSA Stipend Levels" section above for training grant details.
