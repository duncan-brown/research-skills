# NSF-Specific Policies

Policies and requirements specific to National Science Foundation awards.

---

## NSF Two-Month Rule (Senior Personnel)

**IMPORTANT:** This is an **NSF policy**, NOT an NIH policy. NIH has no two-month salary limit.

### Policy

**Senior personnel** (PIs, co-PIs, senior/key persons) are limited to **maximum 2 months salary per year** from NSF without special approval.

**Applies to:**
- NSF grants only
- Across ALL active NSF awards combined (not per-project)
- Calendar months for 12-month appointments
- Academic months for academic year appointments

**Does NOT apply to:**
- NIH or other federal agencies (they have different rules)
- Graduate students
- Postdocs
- Research staff
- Non-senior personnel

### Calculating 2-Month Equivalent

**For 8.5-month AY appointments (Syracuse standard):**
- 2 academic months = 2 ÷ 8.5 = **23.5% of AY salary**

**For 12-month calendar appointments:**
- 2 calendar months = 2 ÷ 12 = **16.7% of annual salary**

### Impact on Course Buy-Outs

**One course = 15% of AY salary:**
- 15% × 8.5 months = 1.275 academic months
- **Within 2-month limit** (1.275 < 2.0) ✓

**Two courses = 30% of AY salary:**
- 30% × 8.5 months = 2.55 academic months  
- **EXCEEDS 2-month limit** (2.55 > 2.0) ✗
- Requires justification in proposal and NSF approval in award notice

**Full teaching relief = 80% of AY salary:**
- 80% × 8.5 months = 6.8 academic months
- Far exceeds 2-month limit
- Requires substantial justification and special NSF Program Officer approval

### NSF Policy Reference

This limitation is specified in:
- NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG)
- Chapter II.D.3.a: Salaries and Wages
- Applies to senior personnel on research grants

## NSF Cost Sharing Prohibition

**Voluntary committed cost sharing is PROHIBITED** on NSF proposals unless explicitly required by a program solicitation.

### Key Points

- Line M on the proposal budget form is unavailable unless the program solicitation mandates cost sharing
- NSF Program Officers are **not authorized** to impose or encourage cost sharing
- Using a lower F&A rate than your negotiated rate constitutes voluntary cost sharing and violates NSF policy
- Cost sharing requirements must be authorized by NSF Director, National Science Board, or legislation

### Programs Requiring Cost Sharing

Very few NSF programs require cost sharing. Check the program solicitation carefully.

Current list of programs with cost sharing requirements: https://www.nsf.gov/bfa/dias/policy/

**Major Research Instrumentation (MRI):** Cost sharing requirement **waived for 5 years** per the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022.

### Why NSF Prohibits Voluntary Cost Sharing

- Levels the playing field between well-funded and less-funded institutions
- Prevents institutions from using resources as a competitive advantage
- Ensures merit review focuses on scientific quality, not institutional wealth

## NSF Participant Support Costs

Participant support costs receive special treatment in NSF budgets.

### Definition

Direct costs for individuals participating in training activities but **not employed** by the project:
- Workshop/conference attendees
- Training program participants  
- Summer institute students
- Short course participants

### Budget Categories

- **Stipends:** Cash payments to participants
- **Travel:** Transportation costs for participants
- **Subsistence:** Per diem, lodging for participants  
- **Other:** Registration fees, materials, parking

### Special Rules for NSF

1. **Excluded from MTDC** - No F&A charged (unless specifically authorized in NICRA)
2. **Separate accounting required** - Must track separately post-award
3. **Cannot be rebudgeted** - Requires NSF prior approval to move funds to/from participant support
4. **Participant = non-employee** - If paid as employee (RA, TA), not participant support

### What is NOT Participant Support

- **PI/co-PI attendance** at conferences (use travel budget)
- **Local participants' meals** during workshop (use Other Direct Costs with F&A)
- **Venue rental** for training event (use Other Direct Costs with F&A)
- **Catering** for workshop (use Other Direct Costs with F&A)

The distinction: If the cost benefits the **project** (venue, catering), it's Other Direct Costs. If it benefits the **participant** (their travel, their stipend), it's Participant Support.

## NSF Equipment Threshold

NSF continues to use **$5,000 equipment threshold** (or institutional threshold if lower).

This aligns with Syracuse University's institutional capitalization policy of $5,000.

While 2 CFR 200 was revised in October 2024 to define equipment as ≥$10,000, NSF program guidance and Syracuse's negotiated rate agreement specify $5,000.

**For NSF proposals: Use $5,000 equipment threshold**

## NSF Budget Justification Requirements

### Page Limits

Budget justifications are limited to **5 pages per proposal**.

**Subawards:** Each subaward requires a separate 5-page budget justification.

### Content Requirements

Must justify:
- Personnel effort and rates
- Equipment necessity  
- Travel destinations and purposes
- Other direct costs

## NSF Domestic Travel Definition

**IMPORTANT:** For NSF purposes, domestic travel includes:
- All 50 US states and territories
- **Canada**
- **Mexico**

All other destinations are considered foreign travel.

This differs from other agencies that may classify Canada/Mexico as foreign.

## NSF Award Management

### Progress Reports

- **Annual reports** due 90 days before end of current budget period
- **Final report** due 120 days after award end
- **Project Outcomes Report** due 120 days after award end (public-facing)
- Submit via Research.gov

### Responsible Conduct of Research

NSF requires RCR training for:
- Undergraduates paid from NSF awards
- Graduate students on NSF awards  
- Postdocs on NSF awards

See Syracuse University's RCR training plan: http://osp.syr.edu/Award-Management/compliance---post-award1/responsible-conduct-of-research-rcr.html
