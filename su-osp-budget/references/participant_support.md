# Participant Support Costs Reference

Source: Cornell University "Managing Participant Support Costs Under the Uniform Guidance" + 2 CFR 200

## Definition

**Participant support** is funding provided to enable and encourage individuals to attend conferences or training programs without undue financial burden.

**Participant support costs** (2 CFR 200.75): Direct costs for items such as stipends or subsistence allowances, travel allowances, and registration fees paid to or on behalf of participants or trainees (but **not employees**) in connection with conferences or training projects.

## Who IS a Participant?

A participant is a **non-employee** who is the **recipient, not the provider**, of a service or training associated with a workshop, conference, seminar, symposium, or other short-term instructional or information sharing activity.

**Examples of participants:**
- Students
- Scholars
- Scientists
- Teachers
- Private sector representatives
- Community members
- Members of the public

**Participants are NOT required to provide any deliverable**, other than meeting program requirements (e.g., attendance, testing).

## Who is NOT a Participant?

- ❌ Employees of the grantee institution
- ❌ Advisory board members
- ❌ Interns who have paid appointments
- ❌ Anyone who has a deliverable or is primarily providing a service to the project
- ❌ Consultants
- ❌ Project personnel
- ❌ Staff members of recipient/subrecipient

## Participant Support Cost Categories

| Category | Description | OSP Template Row |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Stipend** | Set amount paid directly to participant | Row 25 |
| **Travel** | Transportation costs to participate | Row 26 |
| **Subsistence** | Housing and meals during program | Row 27 |
| **Fees** | Registration, conference fees | Row 28 (Other) |
| **Other** | Training materials, lab supplies (if approved) | Row 28 (Other) |

**SU Note**: Fees (registration, conference fees) and Other both go into Row 28 "Other" in the OSP template.

### Stipend Details
- Conditions for receiving stipend should be in writing
- Program completion must be attested to by PI
- Rates must be consistently applied across the program
- Pro-rated where appropriate

### Travel Details
- Must follow sponsor guidelines (U.S. flag carrier, coach class, most direct route)
- Must follow university policies
- Sole purpose of trip must be to participate in the project activity
- Field trips for transportation may be allowable
- **Field trips for recreational purposes are NOT permitted**

### Subsistence Details
- Housing and meal expenses necessary for participation
- Must be reasonable and limited to days of attendance
- Local participants are NOT entitled to subsistence/per diem
- **Exception**: Local participants MAY participate in meals/refreshments provided at the meeting
- **Exception**: REU students are entitled to subsistence even if local
- **Exception**: Employees may receive conference meals provided to all attendees

## What PSCs Do NOT Include

- ❌ Guest speaker or lecturer fees
- ❌ Conference support costs (facility rental, A/V equipment)
- ❌ Service or independent contractor agreements
- ❌ Incentives (prizes), memorabilia, or gifts
- ❌ Employee compensation and expenses (except meals as noted above)

## F&A Treatment

**Participant support costs are EXCLUDED from the Modified Total Direct Cost (MTDC) base** - no indirect costs are charged.

## Agency-Specific Requirements

### General Rule (2 CFR 200.456)
Participant support costs are allowable **with prior approval of the Federal awarding agency**.

### NSF
- GC-1 grants approval for categories listed in §75
- Views training of participants as significant component of mission
- **Excluded from MTDC** (no F&A charged)
- **Rebudgeting rules**:
  - Cannot transfer FROM participant support to other categories without written Program Officer approval
  - CAN move budget INTO participant support
  - CAN rebudget BETWEEN PSC categories
- Each meal at conference must be specifically listed in proposal
- Only beverages (not food) allowed for session breaks
- Requires separate account/sub-account for tracking

### NIH (IMPORTANT - DIFFERENT FROM NSF)

Per NIH Grants Policy Statement Section 7.9:

**"Participant Support Costs: Only allowable when identified in specific NOFOs."**

Key differences from NSF:
- **NOT automatically allowed** - Must be explicitly identified in the Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO/FOA)
- **Rebudgeting requires prior approval** - Both INTO and OUT OF PSC requires NIH approval
- **Excluded from MTDC** (same as NSF)

**NIH Training Grants (Kirschstein-NRSA) - SPECIAL CASE:**
- Do NOT use "participant support" terminology
- Use: "trainees," "trainee-related expenses," "trainee travel"
- Different rules apply - see NRSA guidance

### NSF vs NIH Quick Comparison

| Aspect | NSF | NIH |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Default Allowability | Yes (GC-1) | Only if in NOFO |
| F&A Treatment | Excluded from MTDC | Excluded from MTDC |
| Rebudget OUT of PSC | PO approval required | Prior approval required |
| Rebudget INTO PSC | Allowed | Prior approval required |
| Training Grants | Uses PSC terminology | Different terminology |

### Other Agencies
- PSCs need to be part of the funding opportunity or agency rules
- Must be specifically included in the proposal
- Check specific agency guidelines for F&A treatment

## NSF REU (Research Experience for Undergraduates)

### Participant Requirements
- Must be U.S. citizens or permanent residents
- Foreign students may participate but **cannot receive NSF funding**
- Should not be appointed as student employees in PI's lab

### Funding Guidelines
- Stipend: ~$500 per student per week
- Total costs: Generally not exceed **$1,200 per student per week**
- Higher amounts may be allowed for:
  - International activities
  - Field work in remote locations
  - Other exceptional circumstances

### Documentation
- Results must be included in annual project report

## Documentation Requirements

### Stipends
- Written agreement prior to program stating stipend amount
- Stipend rates consistently applied and pro-rated where appropriate

### Subsistence
- Same documentation as stipends, OR
- Receipts, OR
- Previously agreed-upon per diem

### Program Completion
- Documentation required indicating successful completion
- Attendance records for classes/workshops

## Rebudgeting Rules

| Action | NSF | Other Agencies |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| Move FROM PSC to other categories | Prior written approval required | Agency-specific |
| Move TO PSC from other categories | Allowed | Agency-specific |
| Move BETWEEN PSC categories | Allowed | Agency-specific |
| Add PSC where not previously awarded | Change in scope - requires approval | Requires approval |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Paying employees as participants** - Employees cannot be participants (except for conference meals)
2. **Missing prior approval** - PSCs require agency approval before spending
3. **Exceeding REU limits** - NSF REU generally capped at $1,200/student/week
4. **Rebudgeting without approval** - NSF requires written PO approval to move funds OUT of PSC
5. **Missing documentation** - Need written agreements, attendance records, completion attestation
6. **Including ineligible costs** - Speaker fees, AV rental, prizes are NOT PSCs
7. **Foreign participants on NSF** - Cannot receive NSF participant support funding
