# Graduate Student Tuition Remission Policies

College-specific policies for tuition remission on sponsored projects.

## Current Tuition Costs

**Graduate Tuition (per Syracuse University website):**
- **$2,015 per credit hour**
- **Full-time = 18 credits per academic year** (fall and spring)
- **Total annual tuition = $36,270**

## Tuition Remission by College

### College of Arts and Sciences

**Federal Grants with Full On-Campus R&D F&A (49.5%):**
- **100% tuition remission** for graduate research assistants
- College provides full tuition as remitted tuition (applied as credit to student bursar account)
- **PI does NOT budget for tuition** on federal grants
- This is **NOT cost sharing** (it's foregone revenue, not an expenditure)

**Non-Federal Grants:**
- May use non-federal fringe rate (55.0%) which includes tuition component
- OR charge tuition as direct cost (depends on sponsor guidelines)

**Institutional Funds:**
- Non-federal fringe rate (55.0%) covers tuition

### College of Engineering and Computer Science

**Federal Awards:**
- **50% tuition remission** provided by college
- **PI must budget for 50% of tuition** ($18,135 per year)
- Remaining 50% covered by college

**Non-Federal Grants:**
- Similar 50/50 split or use non-federal fringe rate

### Other Schools and Colleges

**School of Education:**
- Does NOT participate in standard tuition sharing arrangement
- PI must budget for **100% of tuition** as direct cost
- Graduate assistants on sponsored projects should budget full tuition

**Other Schools:**
- Typically follow Arts and Sciences model (100% remission on federal with full F&A)
- Confirm with specific school/college dean's office

## Important Distinctions

### Tuition Remission vs. Cost Sharing

**Tuition Remission:**
- College benefit that reduces what PI budgets
- Foregone revenue (not actual expenditure)
- **NOT reported as cost sharing** per 2 CFR 200.306
- Zero sum for college budget

**Cost Sharing:**
- Actual institutional funds committed to project
- Must be verifiable from institutional records
- Must be necessary and reasonable for project
- Must be included in approved budget
- Examples: PI commits 20% effort but charges only 10%; Department provides equipment not charged to grant

### Federal vs. Non-Federal Fringe Rates

**Federal Sponsored (Fund 13):**
- Graduate Assistant rate: **10.8%** (FY26)
- Tuition handled separately via remission

**Non-Federal Sponsored (Fund 13):**
- Graduate Assistant rate: **55.0%** (FY26)
- Higher rate includes tuition remission component
- Use when tuition is allowable but not directly charged

## Calculating Graduate Student Costs

### Arts and Sciences - Federal Grant
```
Stipend: $28,750 (PhD, FY26)
Fringe (10.8%): $3,105
Tuition: $0 (100% remitted by college)
MTDC: $31,855
F&A (49.5%): $15,768
Total: $47,623
```

### Engineering - Federal Grant
```
Stipend: $28,750
Fringe (10.8%): $3,105
Tuition (50%): $18,135
MTDC: $31,855 (tuition excluded)
F&A (49.5%): $15,768
Total: $65,758
```

### School of Education - Federal Grant
```
Stipend: $28,750
Fringe (10.8%): $3,105
Tuition (100%): $36,270
MTDC: $31,855 (tuition excluded)
F&A (49.5%): $15,768
Total: $83,893
```

## When to Ask About College

Always ask which college/school the graduate student is in when:
- Calculating budget for graduate assistants on federal grants
- Comparing federal vs. non-federal fringe rates
- Multi-year budget planning
- Total project cost estimates

The college makes a **$36,270/year difference** in grant costs!
