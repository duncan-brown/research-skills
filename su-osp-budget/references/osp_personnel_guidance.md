# SU OSP Personnel Budget Guidance

Source: Syracuse University Office of Sponsored Programs website

## Compensation Overview

Compensation is payment for services performed on a sponsored project during the period of performance. It includes:
- Salary
- Wages
- Fringe benefits

**Key Principles:**
- Compensation costs are allowable as direct expense when paid effort provides direct benefit to the award
- Level of compensation must be: (1) reasonable, (2) in accordance with appointment letter, (3) consistent with IBS
- Project should be charged in proportion to benefit received (allocability)

## Institutional Base Salary (IBS)

**Definition:** Annual compensation the University pays for an individual's appointment, whether time is spent on research, teaching, service, or other activities.

### IBS Includes:
- Regular salary
- Secondary assignments (e.g., department chair)
- University service

### IBS Excludes:
- Bonuses
- One-time payments
- Incentive pay
- Honoraria
- Extra compensation
- Income earned outside University duties

### Important Rules:
- IBS is established annually in appointment letter regardless of funding source
- All University employees must be budgeted as salary - **cannot be included as consultants or vendors**
- Compensation may NOT increase solely as result of increase in sponsored funding
- When requesting salary on sponsored projects, % effort must be based on IBS

## Salary Escalation

**Standard escalation: 3% per year** for out-years, consistent with historical levels.

Escalations or salaries that deviate from 3% require HR or supervisor approval.

## Effort Commitment Rules

- **Total institutional effort = 100%** regardless of appointment
- No individual may commit more than 100% institutional or summer effort
- No individual may be compensated at rate exceeding annualized IBS
- Increased/decreased effort from original proposal may require sponsor prior approval

### Grant Writing Warning
**Time devoted to grant writing or any other activities that do not directly benefit the project's objectives may NOT be charged to a federal sponsor.**

### Allowable Incidental Activities
Compensation for incidental activities IS allowable when:
1. Work is directly related to sponsored project
2. Authorized in awarded budget OR prior approval obtained
3. Rate of pay is consistent with IBS

Examples:
- Delivering special lectures about ongoing activity
- Writing reports and articles
- Developing/maintaining protocols (human, animal)
- Managing/securing project-specific data
- Coordinating research subjects
- Participating in appropriate seminars
- Consulting with colleagues and graduate students
- Attending meetings and conferences

## Fringe Benefits

Fringe benefit rates are negotiated annually with DHHS (cognizant federal agency).

### Components Covered:
- Health and dental insurance
- Life and disability insurance
- Social Security (FICA)
- Retirement plans
- Workers' Compensation
- Unemployment Insurance
- Salary Continuation Plan
- Sick Leave pay (non-exempt who didn't use sick leave)
- Severance pay
- Employee tuition remission

### Five Federally Negotiated Rates:

1. **Faculty (AY)** - Faculty with academic year appointments, regular full/part-time staff, part-time adjunct union faculty with standard benefits

2. **Faculty Summer** - Faculty summer appointments, non-exempt union staff with summer appointments

3. **Temporary** - Temporary employees, part-time non-union faculty

4. **Graduate Assistants** - Two sub-rates (see below)

5. **Adjunct Union (Modified)** - Adjunct Union part-time faculty with modified benefits

### IMPORTANT: RCM Rates vs Sponsored Rates
**University RCM fringe benefit rates are NOT applicable to sponsored programs** - they include items not allowable on federal awards.

## Graduate Assistant Special Rules

### Two Fringe Rates for GAs:

**Federal Rate (~33%):**
- Use on ALL federal awards
- Use on non-federal awards where tuition IS a direct charge
- May be used on some non-federal awards if student hasn't previously received tuition support using non-profit rate

**Non-profit/Foundation Rate (~55%):**
- For non-federal awards where tuition is NOT directly charged but IS allowable
- **Cannot be used if sponsor disallows tuition**
- If this rate is waived, NO tuition may be provided to the student

### GA Summer Appointments

Graduate students who have fall/spring/AY assistantship (any funding source) AND work summer on same/similar activities:
- Classify as: **SU Graduate Assistant - Summer**
- Account: **536259**

### Temporary GA Rate
Temporary employee fringe rate applies to graduate students hired for temporary/hourly wage positions.
- These students do NOT receive health benefits

## NIH Salary Cap

NIH limits salary that can be expensed to an award.

### Calculation Example:
```
Faculty Salary:     $200,000
NIH Cap:           $199,300
Effort on NIH:     10%

Maximum charge = Effort % × NIH Cap
               = 10% × $199,300
               = $19,930
```

### Important Notes:
- Salary above cap is unallowable and must be borne by institutional funds
- **Salary over regulatory caps CANNOT be used for cost sharing**
- Cap applies to the salary portion only, not fringe benefits
- Always use current NIH cap for the applicable fiscal year

## Personnel Cost Estimation Checklist

1. ☐ Identify all individuals who will work on project
2. ☐ Determine level of effort for each (in person-months)
3. ☐ Verify IBS for each individual
4. ☐ Apply NIH salary cap if applicable
5. ☐ Apply 3% annual escalation for out-years
6. ☐ Apply correct fringe benefit rate
7. ☐ For GAs: determine federal vs. non-profit fringe rate based on tuition allowability
8. ☐ Verify no individual exceeds 100% effort
9. ☐ Document justification for salary cap situations
