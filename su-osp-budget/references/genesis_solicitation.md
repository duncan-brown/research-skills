## Award Instrument Types

DOE offers two OTA flavors:
1. **Milestone-based OT** — payments only upon successful completion of negotiated milestones. Budget justification should clearly propose milestones with specific costs. In this case the budget does not need to comply with 2 CFR 200 cost principles.
2. **Cost reimbursement OT** — terms similar to a grant, standard cost principles apply.

## Team Composition Requirements

Phase I teams must include partners from at least **2 of 3 categories**:
1. DOE/NNSA National Laboratory or Scientific User Facility
2. Industry
3. Institute of Higher Education (IHE) / Non-profit / Other

**Industrial partner DOE funding** can be up to **20% of total requested budget**.

**DOE/NNSA Labs cannot be subrecipients.** They submit their own application as team members and get funded directly by DOE. Their budgets should be included in the prime application for review purposes but will be deducted from the award at negotiation.

## Cost Sharing

- **Not required** for basic and applied research at non-profit / IHE entities.
- **For-profit entities** must provide:
  - 20% cost share for R&D activities
  - 50% cost share for demonstration and commercial application tasks
- Some focus areas have additional cost share requirements (see focus area listings).
- Institutional commitments (space, facilities, faculty release time, tuition waivers) are encouraged but are NOT formal cost sharing. Document in Appendix 7 of the Project Narrative, not on the budget.

## Equipment Threshold

The FOA states $10,000 for R&R Budget form purposes, but adds: "unless a different threshold is specified in a negotiated Facilities and Administrative Cost Rate."

**Syracuse University's NICRA specifies $5,000**, so SU should use the **$5,000 threshold** for equipment classification.

## Budget Form Structure (SF424 R&R)

The R&R Budget has these sections per budget period:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **A** | Senior/Key Person — name, base salary, months, requested salary, fringe, total per person |
| **B** | Other Personnel — aggregated by category (postdocs, grad students, undergrads, secretarial, other) |
| **C** | Equipment — individual items ≥$5,000 (per SU NICRA) |
| **D** | Travel — domestic (incl. Canada/Mexico) and foreign, separated |
| **E** | Participant/Trainee Support Costs — tuition/fees, stipends, travel, subsistence, other |
| **F** | Other Direct Costs — materials, publication, consultants, ADP/Computer Services, subawards, rental, A&R, other |
| **G** | Total Direct Costs (A through F) |
| **H** | Indirect Costs — type, rate, base, and funds requested |
| **I** | Total Direct and Indirect Costs |
| **J** | Fee (not applicable for IHEs) |
| **K** | Budget Justification (single file attachment) |

## ADP/Computer Services (Section F, Line 4)

**Cloud computing costs must be included under ADP/Computer Services.** This is explicitly called out in the FOA. When building a Genesis budget, ask the PI if they have cloud computing costs and place them in this category.

On the OSP Budget Template, cloud computing costs should be entered in one of the "Other" fields on the Non-personnel tab with a column T justification noting "ADP/Computer Services" so the cost can be correctly mapped to Section F Line 4 of the SF424.

## Tuition on Genesis Budgets

- Only **remitted tuition** is relevant (Row 41 on OSP template). No tuition scholarships.
- On the SF424, remitted tuition goes in **Section F "Other"** — NOT Section E (Participant/Trainee Support).
- Tuition is **excluded from MTDC** (no F&A applied).

## Budget Justification Requirements

The budget justification must:
- Be organized by budget category order (matching the R&R form sections A–I)
- Show verifiable math (products of quantities × rates)
- Explain any inflationary factors
- List ALL personnel working on the project, even those not funded by this award, with their compensation source
- Include the institutional indirect cost rate agreement (or URL)
- For milestone-based OT: propose milestones with specific costs

## Title Page Budget Table

## Allowable Costs (Explicitly Listed in FOA)

- Faculty buyouts
- Administrative personnel support
- Professional development, training, mentoring of students and junior researchers
- Travel to collaborators and labs, including national lab internship costs
- Fringe benefits per negotiated rates
- Temporary dependent-care costs during travel
- Professional society memberships
- Instrumentation and equipment for research
- Equipment/services for personnel with disabilities
- Stipends and benefits for students and postdocs
- Standards development participation
- Salary for outreach, recruitment, training events
- Materials and supplies including computers and software licenses

## Subaward Handling

- Each subrecipient needs a **separate R&R Subaward Budget** with its own budget justification.
- Use the R&R SUBAWARD BUDGET ATTACHMENT(S) FORM from Grants.gov.
- File naming: up to 10 letters of subrecipient name + ".pdf" (max 50 chars).
- DOE/NNSA Labs are NOT subrecipients — they get funded directly by DOE.
- First $25K of each subaward is included in MTDC (per SU NICRA).

## Pre-Award Costs

Recipients may charge pre-award costs incurred within the **90-day calendar period** immediately preceding the effective date if allowable under 2 CFR 200. Costs beyond 90 days require DOE Agreement Officer approval. Pre-award costs are at the applicant's risk.
