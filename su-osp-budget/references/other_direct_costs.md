# Other Direct Costs Reference

Source: 2 CFR 200 Subpart E - Cost Principles

## Materials and Supplies (Row 34)

### 2 CFR 200.453 - Materials and supplies costs, including costs of computing devices

**(a)** Costs incurred for materials, supplies, and fabricated parts necessary to carry out a Federal award are **allowable**.

**(b)** Purchased materials and supplies must be charged at their **actual prices**, net of applicable credits. Withdrawals from general stores or stockrooms must be charged at their actual net cost under any recognized method of pricing inventory withdrawals, consistently applied. Incoming transportation charges are a proper part of materials and supplies costs.

**(c)** Materials and supplies used for the performance of a Federal award may be charged as **direct costs**. In the specific case of computing devices, charging as direct costs is allowable for devices that are **essential and allocable, but not solely dedicated**, to the performance of a Federal award.

**(d)** Where federally-donated or furnished materials are used in performing the Federal award, such materials will be used without charge.

### Key Points

- **Threshold**: Items under $5,000 are supplies; ≥$5,000 are equipment (per Syracuse's institutional capitalization policy and negotiated rate agreement)
- **Computing devices**: See special rules below
- **F&A**: Materials and supplies are included in MTDC base (F&A applies)
- **Justification**: Encourage **semi-detailed** breakdowns (not just lump sums) as PIs will need this detail for budget justification text

### What Goes in Materials and Supplies

- Lab consumables (chemicals, reagents, glassware)
- Office supplies (if directly related to project)
- Computer software licenses
- Small equipment under $5,000
- Computing devices - **with restrictions** (see below)

### Computer/Computing Device Rules

**Computers are NOT automatically allowable on federal awards.**

Per 2 CFR 200.453(c), computing devices can be charged as direct costs if they are "essential and allocable" to the award. However:

1. **General-purpose computers are NOT allowable** - A computer for a PI that may also be used for teaching, service, or other non-grant activities is NOT allowable as a direct cost.

2. **Project-specific computers ARE allowable** - A computer purchased specifically for a postdoc or graduate student to accomplish project work IS allowable.

**Good example**: "Workstation for postdoc to perform gravitational wave data analysis - $4,500"

**Bad example**: "Laptop for PI - $2,000" (likely used for teaching/service too)

### Justification Best Practices

Encourage PIs to provide **semi-detailed** justifications rather than lump sums:

❌ **Too vague**: "Lab supplies - $5,000/year"

✅ **Better**: "Lab supplies ($5,000/year): chemicals and reagents ($2,500), glassware ($1,000), data storage media ($500), miscellaneous consumables ($1,000)"

This detail will be needed for the Budget Justification document.

## Publication Costs (Row 35)

### 2 CFR 200.461 - Publication and printing costs

**(a)** Publication costs for electronic and print media, including distribution, promotion, and general handling, are **allowable**. These costs should be allocated as indirect costs to all benefiting activities of the recipient or subrecipient if they are not identifiable with a particular cost objective.

**(b)** Page charges, article processing charges (APCs), or similar fees such as **open access fees** for professional journal publications and other peer-reviewed publications resulting from a Federal award are allowable where:

1. The publications **report work supported by the Federal Government**; and
2. The charges are levied **impartially** on all items published by the journal, whether or not under a Federal award.

**(c)** The recipient or subrecipient may charge the Federal award **during closeout** for the costs of publication or sharing of research results if the costs were not incurred during the period of performance of the Federal award. If charged to the award, these costs must be charged to the final budget period of the award, unless otherwise specified by the Federal awarding agency.

### Key Points

- **Open access fees**: Explicitly allowable (APCs)
- **Page charges**: Allowable if levied impartially
- **Timing**: Can be charged during closeout even if incurred after performance period
- **F&A**: Publication costs are included in MTDC base (F&A applies)

### What Goes in Publication Costs

- Open access / article processing charges (APCs)
- Page charges for journal articles
- Color figure charges
- Supplementary material fees
- Preprint server fees (if applicable)

### What Does NOT Go Here

- General copying and postage (indirect cost)
- Printing of promotional materials (may be unallowable)
- Non-peer-reviewed publications (check allowability)
