# Budget Justification Templates

Standard text for budget justifications in grant proposals.

## Indirect Costs Statement

Use this template in budget justifications. Replace bracketed text with appropriate values based on project type:

**Standard F&A Statement:**

"Indirect Costs are calculated in accordance with Syracuse University's federally negotiated indirect cost rate agreement (Department of Health and Human Services, dated June 4, 2024), which is currently [SELECT RATE] of modified total direct costs (MTDC). Under our current rate agreement, MTDC includes subawards up to the first $25,000 of each subaward per OMB regulations in effect at the time of negotiation. Syracuse University's equipment threshold is $5,000 per our institutional capitalization policy. The Syracuse University fiscal year runs from July 1 to June 30. Syracuse University applies a 3% annual salary escalation for calculating future year costs in grant budgets. Actual rates in place during the time of the award would be charged."

**Rate Selection:**

Choose the appropriate rate based on activity type and location:

- **On-Campus Research:** 49.5%
- **On-Campus Sponsored Training:** 34.0%
- **On-Campus Other Sponsored Activities:** 35.0%
- **Off-Campus All Programs:** 26.0%

### Examples by Sponsor Type

**Research Grant (NSF, NIH R01, DOE, etc.):**
"...which is currently 49.5% of modified total direct costs (MTDC)."

**Training Grant (NIH T32, etc.):**
"...which is currently 34.0% of modified total direct costs (MTDC)."
*Note: NIH training grants are typically limited to 8% F&A, not 34%*

**Off-Campus Project:**
"...which is currently 26.0% of modified total direct costs (MTDC)."

## Fringe Benefits Statement

**For Personnel Budget Justification:**

"Fringe benefit rates are calculated according to Syracuse University's federally negotiated rate agreement with the Department of Health and Human Services (effective 06/04/2024). Current rates for FY26 (July 1, 2025 - June 30, 2026) are:
- Faculty and full-time staff: 29.5%
- Faculty and staff summer appointments: 15.8%
- Graduate assistants (federal sponsors): 10.8%
- Temporary employees: 7.9%

Actual rates in place at the time of expenditure will be charged to the project."

## Salary Escalation Statement

"Syracuse University applies a 3% annual salary escalation for calculating future year personnel costs, consistent with historical trends. Year 1 salaries are based on current rates, with subsequent years escalated accordingly."

## Graduate Student Compensation Statement

**For PhD Graduate Assistant:**

"The graduate research assistant will be compensated at Syracuse University's minimum stipend level of $[28,750 for FY26 / 29,500 for FY27] for a full-time academic year appointment (20 hours/week, 9 months). The student will also receive full tuition remission [for Arts and Sciences] / partial tuition support [for Engineering]. Fringe benefits are calculated at 10.8% for federal sponsored projects."

## Course Buy-Out Statement

**If budgeting course buy-out:**

"The Principal Investigator will buy out [one course / two courses] of teaching to dedicate additional time to this project. The course buy-out is calculated at 15% of academic-year base salary plus applicable fringe benefits and indirect costs, in accordance with Syracuse University's Faculty Course Buy-Out Policy (approved 9/16/25). This buy-out has been approved by the department chair and dean."

**If full teaching buy-out:**

"The Principal Investigator will buy out all teaching obligations to focus exclusively on this project. The teaching buy-out is calculated at 80% of academic-year base salary plus applicable fringe benefits and indirect costs, in accordance with Syracuse University's Faculty Course Buy-Out Policy. This extraordinary arrangement has been approved by the Provost. Service obligations to the University continue during this period."

## Cost Sharing Statement

**If proposing cost sharing:**

"Syracuse University commits [amount or percentage] in cost sharing for this project, consisting of [describe: PI effort, equipment, facilities, etc.]. This cost sharing is verifiable from institutional records, is necessary and reasonable for project success, and meets all requirements of 2 CFR 200.306."

**NIH Salary Cap Cost Sharing:**

"The Principal Investigator's actual salary exceeds the NIH salary cap (currently $228,000 for calendar year 2026). Syracuse University will provide mandatory cost sharing for the salary amount exceeding the cap, plus associated fringe benefits. The cost-shared portion will be charged to institutional funds and is verifiable from University payroll records."

## Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) Definition

**Standard MTDC explanation:**

"Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC) include all direct salaries and wages, applicable fringe benefits, materials and supplies, services, travel, and up to the first $25,000 of each subaward per our current federally negotiated rate agreement (DHHS, dated June 4, 2024). MTDC excludes equipment (items with per-unit cost ≥$5,000 per Syracuse's institutional capitalization policy), capital expenditures, charges for patient care, rental costs, tuition remission, scholarships and fellowships, participant support costs, and the portion of each subaward in excess of $25,000."

## Sponsor-Specific Variations

### NIH Training Grants (T32, F31, etc.)

"Indirect costs for this training grant are limited to 8% of modified total direct costs, in accordance with NIH training grant policies. Trainee stipends follow NIH NRSA stipend levels ($28,788 for predoctoral trainees). Tuition support is limited to 60% of the requested amount, up to $16,000 per year for predoctoral trainees."

### Sponsors with F&A Caps

"While Syracuse University's federally negotiated F&A rate for on-campus research is 49.5%, [Sponsor Name] limits indirect costs to [X%] of [total direct costs / modified total direct costs]. This proposal uses the sponsor's mandated rate of [X%]."

### Off-Campus Projects

"This project will be conducted primarily off-campus [describe location/facility]. In accordance with Syracuse University's rate agreement, the off-campus F&A rate of 26.0% applies when the majority of both expenditures and project duration (>50%) occur off-campus."
