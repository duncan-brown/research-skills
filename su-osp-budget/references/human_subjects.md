# Human Subject Payments (Row 43)

## Overview

Row 43 is for payments to **research subjects** - individuals participating in research studies covered by human protections regulations. This is distinct from Participant Support Costs (Rows 25-28) which cover participants in workshops, conferences, or training activities.

Syracuse University breaks out Human Subject payments separately because the **Office of Sponsored Accounting (OSA) escrows these funds until the IRB has approved the study**.

---

## Regulatory Basis

### 2 CFR 200.456 (Participant Support Costs)

Human subject payments fall under the broader category of payments for "time and inconvenience." These costs must be:
- **Reasonable** - not so large as to constitute undue influence
- **Allowable** - permitted by the sponsor
- **Properly documented** - as part of the research budget

### IRB Approval Required

The IRB must approve incentive amounts to ensure they are:
- Not coercive
- Not constituting undue enticement to participate
- Appropriate for the participant population
- Reasonable compensation for time and inconvenience

**CRITICAL: No expenditures until IRB approval is obtained.**

## Tax Reporting Requirements

### US Citizens and Permanent Residents

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Form required | W-9 (provides SSN and permanent address) |
| Tax withholding | None |
| IRS reporting | Form 1099-MISC if ≥$600 in calendar year |
| Under $600 | Still taxable income to recipient, but not reported |

### Non-Resident Aliens

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Visa verification | Must have visa permitting such payments |
| Consult | Slutzker Center for International Services |
| Form required | W-8BEN (or W-9 if US person for tax purposes) |
| Tax withholding | **30%** (unless tax treaty exception) |
| IRS reporting | Form 1042-S |
| Outside US | Payments for services performed outside US are not taxable/reportable |

**Tax treaty exception:** If treaty exists between individual's country and US with provision for such payments, withholding may be reduced or eliminated if individual meets treaty terms (length of stay, payment amount, etc.).

## Budget Planning

### What to Include

- Cash payments to research subjects
- Gift cards (treated as cash equivalent)
- Other monetary incentives
- Value of non-monetary incentives (if significant)

### Estimating Costs

Calculate based on:
- Number of subjects needed
- Payment per subject (or per visit/session)
- Number of visits/sessions per subject
- Attrition/dropout rate (budget conservatively)

**Example:**
```
100 subjects × $50/session × 3 sessions = $15,000
Add 20% for recruitment/attrition buffer = $18,000
```

### F&A Treatment

Human Subject payments are **included in MTDC** (F&A applies).

This is different from Participant Support Costs (Rows 25-28) which are excluded from MTDC for NSF and NIH.

## Distinction: Human Subjects vs. Participants

| Factor | Human Subjects (Row 43) | Participants (Rows 25-28) |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Definition | Individuals in research studies | Attendees at workshops/conferences/training |
| Regulations | Human protections regulations apply | Not subject to human protections |
| Consent | Informed consent required | No informed consent required |
| IRB | IRB approval required | No IRB required |
| F&A | Included in MTDC | Excluded from MTDC (NSF/NIH) |
| Account | 561104 | Various |
| OSA handling | Escrowed until IRB approval | Standard processing |

**Key point:** If individuals are being studied (their responses, behaviors, samples analyzed for research purposes), they are **research subjects**. If they are receiving training or education at a workshop/conference, they are **participants**.

Include in justification:
- Number of subjects
- Payment amount per subject (or per visit)
- Number of visits (if longitudinal study)
- Brief rationale for payment level

**Example justification:**
"Payments to research subjects: 150 participants × $25 incentive for completing 45-minute survey = $3,750. Payment level is consistent with IRB-approved protocols for similar survey studies at SU."

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Forgetting IRB timing**: Budget is approved, but funds are escrowed until IRB approval. Plan IRB submission timeline accordingly.

2. **Underestimating attrition**: For longitudinal studies, budget for dropout. A 20% buffer is often reasonable.

3. **Non-resident alien complexity**: 30% withholding significantly impacts effective payment. May need to gross up payments or verify treaty status early.

4. **Gift card tracking**: Gift cards are cash equivalents and subject to same reporting requirements. Maintain records of distribution.

5. **$600 threshold per person**: Track cumulative payments to individuals across calendar year, not project year.
