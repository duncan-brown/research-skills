# SU OSP Non-Personnel Budget Guidance

Source: Syracuse University Office of Sponsored Programs website

## Travel (Rows 19-20)

### What's Included
- Transportation (airfare, ground transportation, mileage)
- Food and lodging
- Conference registration
- Other travel-related expenses

### What's NOT Allowed
- Entertainment costs
- Personal items
- Family member travel (except long-duration research abroad - check sponsor guidelines)

### Budget Justification Requirements
Explain in detail:
- Number of travelers
- Round-trip economy airfare
- Ground transportation costs
- Conference registration (if applicable)
- Number of nights lodging
- Meals (if not included in registration)

### Cost Estimation
- **Airfare**: Use Travelocity or similar to estimate economy fares
- **Lodging/Meals**: GSA per diem rates can assess reasonableness (SU policy doesn't require GSA rates, but sponsors may)
- **Mileage**: Follow IRS rates (updated annually)
- **Ground transportation**: Use website estimates for cabs, parking, transit

### Foreign Travel Rules
- **Definition varies by sponsor**: NSF considers Canada and Mexico as domestic
- **Federal awards**: Must use US air carriers or code-share arrangements (Open Skies Agreement exceptions apply; not applicable to defense sponsors)

## Participant Support Costs (Rows 25-28)

### NSF-Specific Rules
- For participants/trainees (NOT employees) in conferences, meetings, symposia, training, workshops
- **Excluded from MTDC** for NSF
- Non-SU employees in this category are NOT consultants
- Teacher compensation may be governed by union contracts

### NIH-Specific Rules (DIFFERENT FROM NSF)
Per NIH GPS Section 7.9: **"Participant Support Costs: Only allowable when identified in specific NOFOs."**

- **NOT automatically allowed** - Must be explicitly in the NOFO/FOA
- **Excluded from MTDC** (same as NSF)
- Rebudgeting INTO or OUT OF PSC requires NIH prior approval
- NIH Training Grants (NRSA) use different terminology: "trainees," "trainee-related expenses"

### Other Federal Agencies
- PSCs are **NOT excluded from MTDC** for most other federal agencies
- Check specific agency guidelines

### PI Requirements
- Clearly define what participants must do to receive payment
- Maintain documentation of satisfactory completion

### Participants vs. Research Subjects
- **Participants**: Not subject to human subjects protection regulations or informed consent
- **Research Subjects**: Covered by human protections regulations (see Row 43)
- Distinguish clearly in budget narrative
- SU doesn't distinguish internally, so documentation must make purpose clear

## Materials and Supplies (Row 34)

### Categories to List
- Laboratory materials (gases, plasticware, chemicals/reagents, cell culture media, restriction enzymes, radioactive reagents)
- Animals and per diem (**requires IACUC approval**)
- Instrumentation under $5,000 (tape recorders, digital cameras, GPS)
- Desktop/laptop computers (with restrictions - see below)

### Computer/Computing Device Rules (IMPORTANT)

**Computers must be:**
- Exclusively or predominantly used for research/project purpose
- Essential to meet project objectives
- Well justified

**Predominant Use = 75% or greater**
- If ≥75% project use → Charge full cost to project
- If <75% project use → Charge proportional cost only

**Allocation options:**
- Full charge if ≥75% project use
- Proportional charge based on projected usage
- Split between multiple funding sources

## Collaborating with Others (Consultants & Subawards)

### Subrecipient vs. Contractor/Consultant

| Subrecipient | Contractor/Consultant |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Self-directed work, minimal SU direction | SU buying a service with high direction |
| Programmatic decision making responsibility | Provides goods/services within normal operations |
| Performance measured against sponsor objectives | Operates in competitive environment |
| Uses funds for public purpose of award | Services ancillary to award operations |
| Responsible for prime award requirements | Provides benefit to Syracuse University |

### Subrecipient Proposal Requirements
1. Subrecipient Commitment Form OR signed letter of intent from authorized official
2. Required application documents (C&P, biosketches, etc.)
3. Statement of Work including:
   - PI name(s)
   - Period of performance
   - Staffing needs
   - Objectives and measurable tasks
   - Milestones
   - Special resources
   - Meeting/reporting schedule
4. Budget and Budget Narrative

### Consultant Proposal Requirements
1. Letter of intent (5 business days before submission) describing:
   - Service to be provided
   - Cost (quote, published rates, or breakdown)
   - Number of days of service
   - Daily rate
   - Whether travel separately itemized

### Budget Clarity
**Critical**: Agree whether budget amounts are:
- Total costs (direct + indirect), OR
- Direct costs only

## Publication Costs (Row 35)

### Allowable Costs
- Page charges
- Open access fees
- Photography and graphics (if essential for publication)

### Timing Consideration
Is your pace of publication such that costs are reasonable in Year 1? Budget accordingly.

## Research Subjects (Row 43 - Human Subject)

### Definition
Individuals whose involvement is covered by human protections regulations.

### Purpose of Payment
- Enable participation
- Reflect time donated to research
- Must be modest (not coercive or undue enticement)

### Requirements
- **IRB approval required** before any expenditures
- Payments >$600/year to one individual require IRS 1099 reporting

### Account Number
- 561104 Sponsored Subjects

### Distinction from Participants
- Research subjects = covered by human protections, require informed consent
- Participants = not subject to human protections regulations

## Tuition (Rows 41-42)

### Remitted Tuition (Row 41)
Graduate research assistants may be entitled to up to **12 hours of remitted tuition per semester**.

If allowable, charge as "Other Direct Cost."

### Dean's Contribution
The Dean may contribute **up to half** of required tuition. May be contingent on F&A rate used.

### Approval Process

**Arts & Sciences (A&S):**
- Up to half tuition **automatically authorized** for grants at full F&A
- Note credits needed on IRR Question #3
- Dean's signature NOT required for this purpose

**All Other Schools/Colleges:**
- Dean's approval documented on IRR Question #3
- Requires Dean's signature

### Scholarship Tuition (Row 42)
Same approval procedures, EXCEPT:
- A&S Dean or designee MUST sign IRR or send email confirmation to OSP
- Used for individual fellowships (NIH NRSA, NASA) or institutional training grants

### Cost-Sharing Warning
**OSP strongly recommends NOT using tuition for cost-sharing:**
- Actual tuition charged is highly unpredictable
- Unmet cost-sharing must be covered from other sources

If institutional resources contribute to tuition, sponsor and institution charged equal amounts concurrently.

### Tuition Rates
- Budget at rates anticipated during expenditure
- Use OSP budget template rates
- If rate unavailable, increase last rate by **8.16% per year**

### Budget Justification Language
- Describe as "institutional contribution"
- Do NOT include dollar value
- Do NOT identify as "cost-sharing"

### Non-Federal Sponsors
**If tuition is not allowable, the higher fringe benefit rate (55%) cannot be used.**

## Program Income

### Definition
Gross income directly generated by supported activity or earned as result of award.

### Examples
- Fees for services performed
- Rental of property acquired under award
- Conference registration fees
- Sale of commodities/items fabricated under award
- License fees and royalties
- Interest on loans made with award funds

### Requirements
- Indicate expected program income on IRR form
- Federal awards: Must be separately accounted for
- Most federal sponsors: Add to project funds
- Check award terms for disposition rules
