## Consultant Services (Row 36)

### Definition

Per 2 CFR 200.459, "Costs of professional and consultant services rendered by persons who are members of a particular profession or possess a special skill and who are not officers or employees of the recipient or subrecipient are allowable."

**Key characteristics:**
- Individual (not an organization providing routine services)
- Possesses special skill or professional expertise
- NOT an SU employee
- Provides advisory/expert services related to the project
- Works independently (controls HOW work is performed)

### SU Process

From OSP guidance:

**PI/Project Staff Responsibilities:**
1. Select Consultant and document process per University procurement policies
2. Submit Consulting Agreement with attachments outlining:
   - The service to be provided
   - The number of days of service involved (per year, if applicable)
   - The daily rate of compensation
   - Whether travel will be separately itemized
3. Setup Consultant Commitment and process appropriate invoices

**OSP Responsibilities:**
- Review submitted documentation
- Assure that the Consulting Agreement is complete and fully executed

### Rate Reasonableness

Per 2 CFR 200.459(b), the following factors determine allowability:

1. **Nature and scope** of service rendered in relation to service required
2. **Necessity** of contracting for the service, considering the recipient's capability
3. **Past pattern** of such costs, particularly in years prior to Federal award
4. **Impact of Federal awards** on the recipient's business
5. **Whether proportion of Federal work** influences the recipient to incur the cost
6. **Whether service can be performed more economically** by direct employment
7. **Qualifications** of the individual and **customary fees charged**, especially on non-federally funded activities
8. **Adequacy of the contractual agreement** (description, time estimate, rate, termination provisions)

### Agency-Specific Rate Limits

| Agency | Daily Rate Limit | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| **NSF** | No limit (since FY06) | Must be "reasonable" |
| **NIH** | No published limit | Must be "reasonable" |
| **DOD** | Generally no limit | Subject to cost reasonableness |
| **NASA** | Executive Level IV | Tied to Federal pay scale |
| **DOJ** | IBS/260 | For university-affiliated consultants |
| **NSF SBIR/STTR** | $600/day | Specific to small business programs |

**Reasonableness standard:** Payment should be comparable to normal or customary fees charged and received by the consultant for comparable services, especially on non-government contracts and grants.

### Retainer Fees

Per 2 CFR 200.459(c): "To be allowable, retainer fees must be supported by evidence of bona fide services available or rendered in addition to the factors in paragraph (b)."

### Budget Justification Elements

For consultant budget justification, include:
- Consultant name (if known) or "TBD - [expertise area]"
- Specific services to be provided
- Number of days
- Daily rate with reasonableness justification
- Travel (if separate)

**Example:**
"Dr. Jane Smith, expert in gravitational wave data analysis, will provide 5 days of technical consultation on LIGO data processing algorithms at $750/day. Dr. Smith's rate is consistent with her consulting fees charged to other academic institutions for comparable services. Total: $3,750."

### F&A Treatment

Consultant services are **included in MTDC** (F&A applies to the full amount).

## Purchased Services / Contractor (Row 37)

### Definition

Per 2 CFR 200.331, a contract is "for the purpose of obtaining goods and services for the recipient's or subrecipient's use and creates a procurement relationship with a contractor."

**Key characteristics:**
- Provides goods/services within normal business operations
- Provides similar goods/services to many different purchasers
- Operates in a competitive environment
- Provides goods/services ancillary to the project (not programmatic)
- Not subject to Federal program compliance requirements

### Distinction from Consultant

| Factor | Consultant | Contractor/Vendor |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Relationship | Advisory, expert | Service provider |
| Work control | Independent | May have direction |
| Deliverable | Expertise/advice | Goods/services |
| Competition | May be sole source | Competitive environment |
| Operations | Project-specific | Normal business |
| F&A | Included in MTDC | Included in MTDC |

### SU Process

From OSP guidance:

- **Managed by Department Staff** (NOT OSP)
- Must follow University Procurement Policies
- Work with the Purchasing Department
- OSA (Office of Sponsored Accounting) will pre-audit expenditures on sponsored projects

### Procurement Methods (2 CFR 200.320)

| Method | Threshold | Requirements |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Micro-purchase** | ≤$10,000 (up to $50K self-cert) | No competitive quotes required if price reasonable |
| **Simplified acquisition** | $10K-$250K | Price/rate quotations from adequate number of sources |
| **Sealed bids** | >$250K | Public solicitation, lowest price |
| **Competitive proposals** | >$250K | RFP process, evaluation criteria |
| **Noncompetitive** | Any | Only with justification (sole source, emergency, etc.) |

### Common Purchased Services

- **Core facilities** (sequencing, mass spec, imaging)
- **Equipment repair and maintenance**
- **Analytical testing services**
- **Data processing services**
- **Equipment fabrication**
- **Printing and reproduction**
- **Survey services**
- **Translation services**

### Budget Justification Elements

For purchased services, include:
- Description of service
- Vendor/provider (if known)
- Basis for cost estimate
- Competitive procurement statement (if applicable)

**Example:**
"DNA sequencing services at the SU Molecular Analysis Core Facility: 500 samples at $25/sample = $12,500. Rate is the established SU internal recharge rate."

### F&A Treatment

Purchased services are **included in MTDC** (F&A applies to the full amount).

## Rental/Lease Costs (Row 39)

### Regulatory Basis - 2 CFR 200.465

**(a) General allowability:** Rental costs are allowable to the extent that rates are reasonable considering:
- Costs of comparable rental properties
- Market conditions in the area
- Alternatives available
- Type, life expectancy, condition, and value of property leased

Rental arrangements should be reviewed periodically to determine if circumstances have changed and if other options are available.

### Arm's-Length Lease

A lease with an unrelated third party where neither party can control or substantially influence the other.

**Allowable costs:** Reasonable rental payments per the lease agreement.

**Unallowable under arm's-length:** Ownership-type expenses (depreciation, taxes, property insurance, maintenance) - these are the landlord's responsibility.

### Less-Than-Arm's-Length Lease

Per 2 CFR 200.465(c), a less-than-arm's-length lease is one where one party can control or substantially influence the actions of the other.

**Examples include leases between:**
1. Divisions of the recipient or subrecipient
2. The recipient and another entity under common control (common officers, directors, members)
3. The recipient and a director, trustee, officer, key employee, or their immediate family members

**Allowable costs (limited to):**
- Depreciation
- Maintenance
- Taxes (if not exempt)
- Insurance

**Family members defined:**
- Spouse and parents thereof
- Children and spouses thereof
- Parents and spouses thereof
- Siblings and spouses thereof
- Grandparents and grandchildren and spouses thereof
- Domestic partner and parents thereof
- Any individual related by blood or affinity with equivalent close association

### Sale and Leaseback (200.465(b))

Rental costs under "sale and leaseback" arrangements are allowable only up to the amount that would have been allowed if the recipient had continued to own the property (depreciation, maintenance, taxes, insurance).

### Finance Lease (200.465(d))

Rental costs under leases required to be accounted for as a financed purchase under GASB or finance lease under FASB are allowable only up to the amount that would have been allowed if the recipient had purchased the property on the date the lease was executed.

**Unallowable costs include:** Profit, management fees, and taxes that would not have been incurred if purchased.

Interest costs may be allowable if they meet the criteria in § 200.449.

### Operating Lease (200.465(e))

Rental or lease payments are allowable under lease contracts where the recipient is required to recognize an intangible right-to-use lease asset under GASB or right-of-use operating lease asset under FASB.

### Home Office Space (200.465(f))

**UNALLOWABLE:** The rental of any property owned by individuals or entities affiliated with the recipient for purposes such as home office.

### SU Example: Burton Blatt Institute / SE-ADA Center

The Burton Blatt Institute rents space for the South East ADA Center funded by HHS. This is an example of:
- **Arm's-length lease** (third-party landlord)
- **Program-required off-site presence** (regional center)
- **Allowable as direct cost** (directly benefits the project)

### Budget Justification Elements

For rental/lease costs, include:
- Description of space/property
- Location
- Square footage (if applicable)
- Monthly/annual rate
- Justification of need for project
- Statement of reasonableness (comparable rates, market conditions)

**Example:**
"Office space for SE-ADA Center regional operations in Atlanta, GA: 2,500 sq ft at $25/sq ft/year = $62,500/year. Rate is consistent with Class B office space in the Atlanta metropolitan area per CBRE market report."

### F&A Treatment

Rental/lease costs are **included in MTDC** (F&A applies).

## Decision Tree: Consultant vs. Contractor vs. Subrecipient

```
Is the entity an individual with specialized expertise?
├── YES: Does the individual provide advisory services specific to this project?
│   ├── YES → CONSULTANT (Row 36)
│   └── NO: Does individual provide routine services to many clients?
│       ├── YES → CONTRACTOR/Purchased Services (Row 37)
│       └── NO → Likely CONSULTANT (Row 36)
│
└── NO: Is the entity carrying out a substantive portion of the project?
    ├── YES: Does entity have programmatic decision-making responsibility?
    │   ├── YES → SUBRECIPIENT (Subaward, Rows 70-88)
    │   └── NO: Is entity measured against project objectives?
    │       ├── YES → SUBRECIPIENT
    │       └── NO → CONTRACTOR (Row 37)
    │
    └── NO: Does entity provide routine goods/services?
        ├── YES → CONTRACTOR/Purchased Services (Row 37)
        └── NO → Evaluate further - may be subrecipient
```

### Key Questions for Classification

1. **Who controls HOW the work is done?**
   - Entity controls → Subrecipient or Consultant
   - SU controls → Contractor

2. **What is being provided?**
   - Programmatic work toward objectives → Subrecipient
   - Expert advice/consulting → Consultant
   - Routine goods/services → Contractor

3. **Is this the entity's normal business?**
   - Specialized for this project → Subrecipient or Consultant
   - Normal operations → Contractor

4. **Is the entity subject to Federal compliance?**
   - Yes → Subrecipient
   - No → Contractor
